## ✨ Your Challenge ✨

Design a **3D desk organizer** in **Tinkercad**.

At the end, present a short tech demo.

Your design should solve real problems and reflect the kind of user you choose.

---

## Design Brief

Your organizer must:

- ✏️ Hold writing tools
- 📎 Hold small objects
- ⭐ Hold one personal item

Good design isn't just making objects.

**It's making objects that shape people and experiences.**


---

## Step 1 — Choose a User Type

Pick a type of student to design for. Be prepared to explain your choice to the class.

- 🌀 Messy Creator
- ⚡ Multi-Tasker
- 🧘 Minimalist
- ✍️ Custom (describe in five words)

Your design should match your user.

---

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 2 — Look at Your Desk</strong></summary>

<pre>

Ask yourself:

• What do I use most?
• What gets lost?
• What annoys me?

</pre>

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 3 — Make a Quick Plan</strong></summary>

<pre>

Decide:

• Where will things go?
• What shapes will you use?

Sketch if it helps.

</pre>

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 4 — Get Feedback</strong></summary>

<pre>

Before building, show your idea to classmates.

Collect feedback from at least three students.

Find:

• Three strengths (pros)
• Three weaknesses (cons)

What surprised you?

What changes will you make?

</pre>

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 5 — Build in Tinkercad</strong></summary>

<pre>

Start simple:

• Big shapes first
• Compartments second
• Details last

Tools you may need:

• Shapes
• Resize
• Move
• Duplicate

</pre>

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 6 — Test Your Design</strong></summary>

<pre>

Ask yourself:

• Does it match my user type?
• Does it solve a real problem?
• Would I actually use this?
• Did feedback improve the design?

</pre>

</details>

---

## Tech Demo

⏱️ **90 seconds maximum**

Be ready to explain:

- Your user type
- One design decision you made for your user
- How feedback changed your design
- Why it matters


## Final Thought

Objects shape people and experiences. What objects have shaped you?
